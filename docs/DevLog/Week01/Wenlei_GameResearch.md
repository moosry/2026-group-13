# Reference Game 1: **Casual Games**

**Examples:** *Fruit Ninja, Merge Big Watermelon, Cut the Rope, Where’s My Water?*

## Feature 01: Instant Visual Feedback
- Provide high frequency, low latency visual responses with particle effects, burst animations, and other expressive visuals.
- ***Why players love it ?*** Enhance tactile satisfaction to maintain the player’s action rhythm.

## Feature 02: Physics Simulation
- These games rely on authentic physical behaviors: gravity, inertia, collisions, fluid dynamics, and more.
- ***Why players love it ?*** Physical feedback strengthens immersion and makes interactions feel credible.

## Feature 03: Approachable Visual Style
- Soft, friendly visual language creates a relaxed emotional atmosphere.
- ***Why players love it ?*** Cute characters and flexible animations increase emotional delight.

## Feature 04: Mass Market Appeal
- Simple and explicit mechanics
- ***Why players love it ?*** Lowering the entry barrier encourages players to continue playing.

# Reference Game 2: **Environmental Narrative Games**

**Example:** *INSIDE, Little Nightmares*

## Feature 01: Highly Immersive Non Verbal Storytelling
- Minimal dialogue or text; storytelling is delivered through environments, lighting, and character actions.
- ***Why players love it ?*** Players actively interpret the world through exploration, deepening immersion and engagement.

## Feature 02: Visual Metaphors and Symbolic Systems
- INSIDE: factories, pursuers, experimental subjects  
- Little Nightmares: the long armed janitor, grotesque chefs
- ***Why players love it ?*** Triggering players interpretive curiosity, engaging subconscious emotions.

## Feature 03: Deep Integration of Puzzles and Narrative
- Puzzle mechanics function as narrative carriers, allowing gameplay and storytelling to strengthen each other.
- ***Why players love it ?*** Giving them the sense that they are uncovering the story through their own actions, creating stronger immersion.
